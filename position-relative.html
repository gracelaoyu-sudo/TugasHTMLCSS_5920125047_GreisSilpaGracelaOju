The Evolution of the Bicycle

In 1817 Baron von Drais invented a walking machine that would help him get around the royal gardens faster:two same-size in-line whells,the front one steerable,mounted in a frame upon which you straddiet.The device was propelied by pushing your feet againt the grount,thus rolling yourself and the device forward in a sort of gliding walk

The machine became known as the drasienne (or "hobby horse") it was made entirely of wood this enjoyed a short lived popularity as a fad not being paractical for transpotacion in anty other place than a well mainthened patway such as in a park or gargen

The next appearance of a two whwled niding machin was in 1865 when pedals were appiled directy to the front whel this machine was knoen as the velocipede (meaning "fast fool")as well as the "bone shaker" since it's wooden structure combined with the cobelston road of the day made for an extrenely uncofortable ride they also became a fad and indoor riding academis similiar to roller rinks could be found in large cities.
